Christmas, a holiday that's usually celebrated on December 25, is an annual holiday for the birth of Jesus as a religious and cultural celebration among billions of people. All of the related holidays are Christmastide, Christmas Eve, Advent, Annunciation, Epiphany, Baptism of the Lord, Nativity Fast, Nativity of Christ, Old Christmas, Yule, St. Stephen's Day, and Boxing Day. Christmas is celebrated on Dec. 25 is for Western Christianity and part of the Eastern churches, Jan. 7 for Most Oriental Orthodox and part of the Eastern Orthodox churches, Jan. 6 for Armenian Apostolic Church and the Armenian Evangelical Church, and Jan. 19 for Armenian Patriarchate of Jerusalem.
